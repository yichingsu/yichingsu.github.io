Current project

L2 Acquisition of Anaphora and Pronoun Resolution


This study aims to investigate the following two research issues in L2 acquisition from a cross-linguistic perspective:

1. How the locality and orientation conditions of the target L2 are acquired by (1) L1 English L2 Mandarin learners; (2) L1 Mandarin L2 English learners; (3) L1 Japanese L2 Mandarin learners? Specifically, we intend to inspect how the different properties regarding these two conditions in English and Mandarin influence the process of L2 acquisition, and whether sharing similar properties as in Mandarin and Japanese will facilitate the target L2 acquisition. We also plan to take a closer look at the pattern from each L2 learner to see whether there is any implication relationship regarding the acquisition of these two conditions in order to examine the plausibility of the UG principle approach and the feature-based approach.

2. How forward/backward anaphora sentences with overt subject are interpreted by (1) L1 English L2 Mandarin learners; (2) L1 Mandarin L2 English learners; (3) L1 Spanish L2 Mandarin learners? Specifically, we ask whether the learner’s L1 is a null-subject language influence the acquirability of backward anaphora for overt subject in their L2 in order to test the validity of the predictions made by the Interface Hypothesis.

Recently completed project

Felicity condition and children’s knowledge of restrictive focus

The goal of this study is to investigate the development of the knowledge for restrictive focus through experiments. Previous studies on children’s interpretations of sentences with restrictive focus predominantly found children assign non-adult interpretations to these sentences, regardless of the languages. However, the error patterns and the degree of variation from adult interpretations were not consistent among those studies. Moreover, the only study that examined children’s use of restrictive focus found Cantonese children started to use it as early as age 3. This raises the possibility that the non-adult patterns found in those comprehension studies were in fact resulted from inappropriate experimental design which did not fulfill the felicity condition required for the use of restrictive focus. Therefore, the study will include two parts. The experiments were designed to incorporate the felicity condition in order to inspect children’s knowledge of restrictive focus for sentences with pre-subject ‘zhiyou’. The findings from this study will provide important information in our understanding of relevant syntactic /semantic interface regarding restrictive focus in child language.
